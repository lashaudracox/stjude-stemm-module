# Cell Division Gone Wrong
### Understanding Cancer at the Cellular Level

**A flipped classroom instructional module for Grades 9–10**
Designed for St. Jude STEMM Education & Outreach · LaShaudra Cox, Instructional Designer

---

## About This Module

This module teaches high school biology students how mutations in cell cycle checkpoint genes lead to uncontrolled cell division — and why that is the biological basis of cancer. Every lesson connects directly to St. Jude Children's Research Hospital's research mission, including real patient narratives, TP53 mutation research, the St. Jude Cloud genomic data portal, and CAR-T cell immunotherapy.

The module uses a **flipped classroom model**: students engage with core content independently through online lessons before class, and face-to-face time is reserved entirely for analysis, case study discussion, and problem solving.

## Lesson Objective

> Students will **analyze** how mutations in cell cycle checkpoint genes lead to uncontrolled cell division and **explain** why this is the biological basis of cancer.

Objective verbs are drawn from Anderson & Krathwohl's (2001) revised Bloom's Taxonomy at the **analysis level**, which sets the cognitive floor for every assessment item in the module.

## Instructional Design Framework

Every design decision in this module was made intentionally, grounded in published research:

| Decision | Theory | Source |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Problem-centered around a patient case (Maya) | First Principles of Instruction | Merrill, 2002 |
| Analogies as cognitive scaffolds (brake/accelerator) | Cognitive Load Theory | Sweller, 2019 |
| Flipped model with exit ticket triage | Formative Assessment Theory | Harris & Jones |
| Guided notes offload organizational load without removing conceptual demand | Scaffolding for PBL | Belland, Kim & Hannafin, 2013 |
| Simulator as step-through application (not passive animation) | First Principles — Application | Merrill, 2002 |
| 4 scenarios in simple-to-complex sequence | 4C/ID & Cognitive Load | van Merriënboer et al., 2009 |
| Printed reference guide eliminates context-switching | Split-Attention Effect | Sweller, 2019; Hollender et al. |
| ARCS framework throughout (attention, relevance, confidence, satisfaction) | Motivational Design | Keller, 1987 |

A full design rationale document — explaining the literature basis behind every deliverable — is available alongside the complete material set.

## Simulator Scenarios

The **Cell Cycle Checkpoint Simulator** includes four interactive scenarios:

| Scenario | Mutation Type | Outcome |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| ✅ Healthy Cell | None | Normal division — two identical daughter cells |
| 🔴 TP53 Mutation | Loss-of-function (tumor suppressor) | G1 checkpoint bypassed → apoptosis via G2 backup |
| 🟡 RAS Oncogene | Gain-of-function (proto-oncogene) | Accelerated division → elevated cancer risk |
| ⚠️ TP53 + RAS Combined | Both mutations | Uncontrolled division → tumor formation |

Each scenario steps through the cell cycle phase by phase with embedded **Think questions** designed to push students from observation to analysis.
