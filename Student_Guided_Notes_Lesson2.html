👤 Section 6 — Case Study: Meet Maya
Patient Case File

"Maya is 12 years old. She was recently diagnosed with Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)."

📋Lab report shows an abnormally high white blood cell count — lymphoblasts are present in large numbers in the blood
🔬Bone marrow biopsy confirms lymphoblasts in uncontrolled division — normal red blood cell production is being crowded out
🧬Genetic panel reveals a mutation in a tumor suppressor gene that normally regulates lymphoblast development
❓The question your team must answer: Which checkpoint failed — and what does the evidence tell you?
Case Analysis Question Your Response
Based on the clues, which cell cycle checkpoint most likely failed in Maya's cells?
What type of mutation caused this — gain-of-function or loss-of-function? How do you know?
Why are Maya's white blood cells specifically affected — not skin or muscle cells?
If you were a St. Jude researcher, what would be the first thing you'd want to know about Maya's specific mutation?
