Key Proteins

p53 (TP53 gene)

Detects DNA damage at G1 checkpoint. Activates repair pathways or triggers apoptosis. Mutated in >50% of cancers.

RAS Protein

Relay protein for growth factor signals. When mutated, stays permanently "active" — signals the cell to divide even without external growth triggers.

Rb Protein

Another tumor suppressor — acts as a "brake" at the G1 checkpoint. When inactivated, cells can enter S phase without proper clearance.

Apoptosis

Programmed cell death. A normal, healthy outcome for cells with too much DNA damage. Cancer cells often develop ways to block apoptosis signals.

St. Jude Connection

TP53 & Pediatric Cancer

St. Jude researchers have studied inherited TP53 mutations in childhood cancer families. Children with Li-Fraumeni syndrome carry one mutated TP53 copy from birth — they need only one more "hit."
